Instructions
For the Dough:
  • 2 ¼ tsp active dry yeast (1 packet)
  • 1 ½ cups warm water (110°F / 43°C)
  • 3 ½ to 4 cups all-purpose flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp olive oil, plus extra for the bowl
For the Sauce:
  • 1 cup canned crushed tomatoes (preferably San Marzano for a rich flavor)
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • A pinch of sugar (optional, to balance acidity)
  • Fresh basil leaves (optional)
For the Toppings:
  • 8 oz fresh mozzarella, thinly sliced or torn into pieces
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Olive oil, for drizzling
Prepare the Dough:
  • In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast in warm water and let it sit for 5-10 minutes until foamy.
  • In a large mixing bowl, combine 3 ½ cups flour and salt. Add the yeast mixture and olive oil.
  • Mix until a soft dough forms, then knead on a lightly floured surface for 8-10 minutes, until smooth and elastic. If sticky, add a bit more flour.
  • Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp towel, and let it rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours, until doubled in size.
Make the Sauce:
  • In a bowl, mix the crushed tomatoes with garlic, salt and olive oil. Add a pinch of sugar if the tomatoes are very acidic.
  • Taste and adjust seasoning. Set aside.
Assemble the Pizza:
  • Preheat your oven to the highest setting (usually 500°F / 260°C) with a pizza stone or baking sheet inside to heat.
  • Punch down the dough and divide it into two equal parts. Roll each piece into a ball and let it rest for 10 minutes.
  • On a floured surface, roll or stretch each dough ball into a 12-inch round. Transfer to a lightly floured pizza peel or baking sheet.
Add Toppings
  • Spread a thin layer of sauce over the dough, leaving a small border for the crust.
  • Add the mozzarella slices evenly on top o fteh sauce.
  • Scatter a few fresh basil leaves and drizzle lightly with olive oil.
Bake:
  • Carefully slide the pizza onto the preheated stone or baking sheet.
  • Bake for 8-10 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly and lightly browned.
  • Remove from the oven, top with more fresh basil if desired, and serve hot
